The Challenge // 19 February 2026

Two Rounds.
One Champion.

A structured battle — from AI awareness to building a working, domain-specific chatbot under pressure.

Round 01

AI Quiz — Basic

// 30 min  ·  Beginner-friendly
  • Basics of Artificial Intelligence and chatbots
  • Introduction to LLMs and how they are used
  • Prompt basics and how prompts affect outputs
  • Fun "AI vs Human" — guess who wrote it!
  • Short puzzles and logical thinking questions
  • Interesting AI facts and knowledge questions
No prior AIML experience required. Beginner-friendly and engaging — filters participants for Round 2.
Round 02

Domain-Specific Chatbot Build

// 3 hours  ·  Build & Demo
  • Receive a domain-specific roblem statement at the start
  • Design and build a domain-specific chatbot
  • Use ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or any LLM
  • Implement meaningful query handling within the domain
  • Support multi-turn conversation (context retention)
  • Handle unclear, irrelevant, or out-of-domain inputs
  • Ensure structured and user-friendly responses
  • Use any tech stack or development tools
  • (Optional) Push to GitHub for bonus consideration
After building, explain your prompt design, model choice, context strategy, and edge case handling during the demo.

Scoring System

Evaluation Criteria

Your chatbot is judged across 7 parameters — 100 points total. Each criterion measures different aspects of AI development excellence.

20%
Critical
Problem Understanding
  • Clarity in understanding the given domain
  • Logical structuring of the solution
  • Practical usefulness of the chatbot
  • Clear system flow (input → processing → response)
10%
Important
Model Selection
  • Choice of LLM (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, etc.)
  • Task suitability of the model
  • Reasoning behind model selection
20%
Critical
Prompt Engineering
  • Clarity and structure of prompts
  • Role definition and instruction design
  • Context handling and conversation continuity
  • Output formatting (tables, bullets, structured responses)
20%
Critical
Accuracy & Relevance
  • Correctness of responses
  • Domain relevance of answers
  • Consistency in responses
  • Ability to handle real user queries effectively
10%
Performance
Latency & Performance
  • Response speed
  • Smooth functioning during demo
  • Stability of the chatbot
10%
Important
Edge Case Handling
  • Handling vague or irrelevant queries
  • Empty/wrong inputs management
  • Fallback responses
  • Out-of-domain or unsafe queries handling
10%
Presentation
Communication & Presentation
  • Clarity in explaining workflow
  • Ability to justify design decisions
  • Demo clarity and confidence
